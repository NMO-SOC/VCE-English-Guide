Thematic Quote Bank

Fame and Obsession

  • Norma Desmond: ’I am big. It’s the pictures that got small.’

  • Norma Desmond: ’They still write me fan letters every day. Why do they beg me for my photographs? Because they want to see me, me, me! Norma Desmond.’

  • Gillis’ Voice: ’She was still sleepwalking along the giddy heights of a lost career — plain crazy when it came to that one subject: her celluloid self, the great Norma Desmond.’

  • Norma Desmond: ’Salome — what a woman! What a part! The Princess in love with a Holy man. She dances the Dance of the Seven Veils. He rejects her, so she demands his head on a golden tray, kissing his cold, dead lips.’

  • Gillis’ Voice: ’I could sense her eyes on me from behind those dark glasses, defying me not to like what I read, or maybe begging me in her own proud way to like it. It meant so much to her.’

  • Norma Desmond: ’I wrote that with my heart.’

  • Norma Desmond: ’I hate that word. It is a return. A return to the millions of people who have never forgiven me for deserting the screen.’

Isolation and Decay

  • Gillis’ Voice: ’A neglected house gets an unhappy look. This one had it in spades. It was like that old woman in Great Expectations — Miss Havisham in her rotting wedding dress.’

  • Gillis’ Voice: ’There was a tennis court, or rather the ghost of a tennis court, with faded markings and a sagging net.’

  • Gillis’ Voice: ’There was something else going on below: the last rites for that hairy old chimp, performed with the utmost seriousness — as if she were laying to rest an only child.’

  • Gillis’ Voice: ’The whole place seemed to have been stricken with a kind of creeping paralysis, out of beat with the rest of the world, crumbling apart in slow motion.’

  • Gillis’ Voice: ’And of course she had a pool. Who didn’t then? It was empty now... or was it?’

  • Gillis’ Voice: ’That night I’d had a mixed-up dream. In it was an organ grinder. I couldn’t see his face, but the organ was all draped in black, and a chimp was dancing for pennies.’

  • Gillis’ Voice: ’She’d sit very close to me, and she’d smell of tuberoses, which is not my favorite perfume, not by a long shot.’

Reality vs. Illusion

  • Norma Desmond: ’I hate that word. It is a return. A return to the millions of people who have never forgiven me for deserting the screen.’

  • Norma Desmond: ’We didn’t need dialogue. We had faces. There just aren’t any faces like that any more.’

  • Gillis’ Voice: ’You don’t yell at a sleepwalker — he may fall and break his neck. That’s it — she was still sleepwalking along the giddy heights of a lost career.’

  • Gillis’ Voice: ’She sure could say a lot of things with those pale eyes of hers. They’d been her trademark. They’d made her the Number One Vamp of another era.’

  • Norma Desmond: ’Still wonderful, isn’t it? And no dialogue. We had faces.’

  • Norma Desmond: ’The plain fact was that she was afraid of that world outside. Afraid it would remind her that time had passed.’

  • Gillis’ Voice: ’She sat coiled up like a watch spring, her cigarette clamped in a curious holder... begging me in her own proud way to like it.’

Power Dynamics

  • Norma Desmond: ’You proud boy, why didn’t you tell me you were having difficulties. It’s all taken care of. It’s all paid for.’

  • Max: ’I made your bed this afternoon.’ (Indicating that Norma has already decided Joe will stay)

  • Norma Desmond: ’Suppose you make up your mind. Do you want this job or don’t you?’

  • Gillis: ’I let him unpack my things. I wanted the dough and I wanted to get out of there as quickly as possible.’

  • Gillis: ’I’m used to paying my own bills.’

  • Norma Desmond: ’Now, now, don’t let’s be small about such matters. We won’t keep books.’

  • Gillis’ Voice: ’So I let him unpack my things. I wanted the dough, and I wanted to get out of there as quickly as possible.’

The Dark Side of Hollywood

  • Gillis: ’Well, I needed about two hundred and ninety dollars real quick, or I’d lose my car... I was way ahead of the finance company.’

  • Norma Desmond: ’Look at them in the front offices — the masterminds! They took the idols and smashed them.’

  • Betty Schaefer: ’It’s from hunger... Just a rehash of something that wasn’t very good to begin with.’

  • Gillis: ’Just sold an original for a hundred grand. The Life of the Warner Brothers. Starring the Ritz Brothers.’ (sarcastically to Skolsky, showing how phony Hollywood is)

  • Sheldrake: ’Who wants to see a Civil War picture?’ (regarding Gone with the Wind)

  • Gillis’ Voice: ’Actors and stock girls and waiters. Kind of a combination office, Kaffee-Klatsch and waiting room. Waiting, waiting for the gravy train.’ (about Schwab’s Drugstore)

  • Gillis’ Voice: ’The whole place seemed to have been stricken with a kind of creeping paralysis, out of beat with the rest of the world, crumbling apart in slow motion.’

Gender Roles

  • JOE A woman almost twice my age ...

  • JOE A very simple set-up: An older woman who is well-to-do. A younger man who is not doing too well ... Can you figure it out yourself?

  • JOE Norma, grow up. You’re a woman of fifty. There’s nothing tragic about being fifty — not unless you try to be twenty-five.

  • GILLIS I don’t need any clothes, and I certainly don’t want you buying them for –

  • NORMA I’ll buy you a boat and we’ll sail to Hawaii.

  • NORMA I own three blocks downtown. I have oil in Bakersfield — pumping, pumping, pumping. What’s it for but to buy us anything we want.

  • NORMA Just follow me.

  • GILLIS Pull up at the drugstore, will you, Max. (To Norma) I’ll get you some.

Love, Infatuation and Narcissism

  • NORMA Salome – what a woman! What a part! The Princess in love with a Holy man. She dances the Dance.

  • GILLIS They’ll love it in Pomona.

  • NORMA They will love it every place. Read it. Read the scene just before she has him killed!

  • NORMA Perfect. Wonderful shoulders. And I love that line.

  • NORMA Is it funny that I’m in love with you?

  • NORMA I’m in love with you. Don’t you know that? I’ve been in love with you all along.

  • NORMA What you’re trying to say is that you don’t want me to love you. Is that it?

  • ARTIE Wait a minute. This is the woman I love. What’s going on? Who was loaded?

  • NORMA To fall in love with you – that was the idiotic thing.

  • BETTY Of course I love him. I always will. I’m just not in love with him any more.

  • JOE And she was in love with me. Me! She was a fool not to sense that there was something phoney in my set-up.

  • NORMA (In a whimpering monotone) I love you, Joe. I love you, Joe. I love you, Joe. I love you, Joe.

  • BETTY If you love me, Joe.

  • NORMA You’re here, Joe ... When did you come home? Where were you? Is it a woman? I know it’s a woman ... Who is she? Oh Joe, why can’t I ask you? I must know, I must!
