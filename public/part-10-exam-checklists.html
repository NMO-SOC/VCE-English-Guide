Exam Checklists

General Checklist for Section A Response

Textual Understanding and Interpretation

  • Have I demonstrated a clear and accurate understanding of the text?

  • Have I explored the ideas, concerns, and values present in the text?

  • Have I considered the structure and language used in the text to convey meaning?

  • Have I shown awareness of the complexities or subtleties in the text’s ideas?

Response to the Topic

  • Have I clearly addressed the essay topic throughout my response?

  • Have I interpreted the implications of the topic (not just the literal meaning)?

  • Have I developed a relevant and thoughtful argument or discussion?

  • Have I used an appropriate strategy to explore the topic (e.g., thematic, character-based, structural)?

Use of Evidence

  • Have I supported my ideas with specific and relevant evidence from the text?

  • Have I explained how my evidence supports my argument?

  • Have I used quotations effectively and accurately?

Organisation and Coherence

  • Is my essay well-structured, with a clear introduction, body, and conclusion?

  • Are my ideas logically sequenced and connected?

  • Have I written clear topic sentences and developed each paragraph?

Language Use

  • Is my language accurate, appropriate, and mostly free of errors?

  • Have I used precise and expressive vocabulary to convey meaning?

  • Is my writing fluent and confident in tone?

General Checklist for Section B Response

Engagement with Ideas and Stimulus

  • Have I considered the ideas raised by the title and stimulus in a thoughtful and meaningful way?

  • Have I connected these ideas to a clear and relevant Framework of Ideas?

  • Have I explored the implications or complexities of the ideas, rather than treating them superficially?

Purpose and Voice

  • Have I clearly established the purpose(s) of my text?

  • Have I maintained an appropriate and consistent voice throughout the piece?

  • Is the form of the piece suited to the intended audience and purpose (e.g., speech, monologue, letter)?

Control of Language

  • Have I used language that is clear, expressive, and appropriate for the form?

  • Have I used a rich and precise vocabulary to strengthen my writing?

  • Have I varied sentence structure and language features to create impact and cohesion?

Control of Text Structure

  • Is my writing well-organised and easy to follow?

  • Have I structured the text purposefully, with a clear beginning, middle, and end?

  • Have I used paragraphs and transitions effectively to guide the reader?

General Checklist for Section C Response

Understanding of Argument and Contention

  • Have I accurately identified the contention of the text?

  • Have I understood how the argument(s) are structured and developed across the text?

  • Have I recognised the overall point of view being expressed by the author/speaker?

Analysis of Persuasive Language and Visuals

  • Have I analysed how language features (written or spoken) are used to persuade the intended audience?

  • Have I explained how visuals (if any) support or enhance the written or spoken argument?

  • Have I discussed how these elements work together to construct the overall persuasive strategy?

Expression and Language Use

  • Have I used language that is clear, precise, and appropriate to an analytical task?

  • Have I written fluently and used analytical vocabulary confidently?

  • Have I maintained a consistent and formal tone suitable for academic analysis?
