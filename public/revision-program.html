Part 13 · Effectively Studying For Exams

Week-by-Week Revision Program

Twelve weeks of structured revision mapping this site’s chapters and tools to a plan. Count back twelve weeks from the exam and start; if you have fewer weeks left, begin at the matching week number and compress the early stages. Adjust around your SACs — the order matters more than the calendar.

Weeks 12–11 · Rebuild the foundations

  • Read: your text’s Background and Context and Values chapters (Sunset Boulevard / Rainbow’s End), then What is Needed in an Essay.
  • Do: re-watch the film / re-read the play once, casually. Skim the scene summaries or quote tables alongside.
  • Tool: 10 minutes of Quote Flashcards daily — start building recall now, when it’s cheap.

Weeks 10–9 · Master the evidence

  • Read: the Thematic and Character Quote Banks for your text, and the Symbols chapters.
  • Do: build a one-page theme map: each major theme, three quotes, one symbol, one technique. The Glossary supplies the technique names.
  • Tool: flashcards daily; add the Technique Quiz twice a week.

Weeks 8–7 · Learn the craft of the essay

  • Read: Planning, Analytical Text Response Structure (the Th.E.S.A.M.L. method), and the Prompt Types chapters for both texts.
  • Study: one annotated exemplar per week (SB / RE) — click through every highlight, one colour category at a time.
  • Tool: Essay Outline Builder — plan two essays per week without writing them. Micro-Drills for topic sentences.

Weeks 6–5 · Write, mark, rewrite

  • Do: one full timed Section A essay per week (Practice Topics & Timer, 60 minutes). Mark it with the Essay Marker or swap with a friend against the criteria.
  • Read: the 2024 and 2025 assessor takeaways — especially the topic-verb guidance.
  • Fix: run a paragraph through the Vocabulary Upgrader after each essay.

Weeks 4–3 · Sections B and C

  • Read: Creating Texts with its three scored sample pieces, and Analysing Argument including the vocabulary bank.
  • Do: one timed Section B piece and one timed Section C analysis per week; mark both with the Essay Marker. Use the practice stimuli and practice articles.

Weeks 2 · Full dress rehearsals

  • Do: at least one complete three-hour exam under conditions — the Exam Generator makes a fresh paper, or use a practice exam. Fifteen minutes reading time, no phone, all three sections.
  • Mark: every section against the criteria; note which section costs you most and give it the spare hours this week.

Week 1 · Final week

  • Read: the Exam Checklists, the Priority Checklist for 2026, and Exam Format one last time.
  • Do: light passes of the quote banks and flashcards; one plan a day with the Outline Builder — no more full essays after mid-week. Sleep is revision.

Pair this program with the advice in the rest of Effectively Studying For Exams — spaced repetition, study-life balance, and exam-day strategy.
