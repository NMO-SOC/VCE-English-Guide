Section A
Analytical Response to a Text
Write an essay responding to a topic about the ideas, concerns and values the author presents. Close reference to the text is required. Marked out of 10.
0 words
Click to upload a photo of handwritten work — Claude will transcribe it
Assessment Criteria:
  • Knowledge and understanding of the text, its structure, and ideas, concerns and values
  • Development of a coherent analysis in response to the topic
  • Use of evidence from the text to support the analysis
  • Fluent expression and appropriate vocabulary / Standard Australian English
Expected Qualities:
9–10Close/perceptive reading; explores complexities; perceptive topic understanding; cogent, well-substantiated discussion; precise/expressive language
8Thoughtful reading; explores ideas through structure/language; understands implications; detailed, coherent discussion; fluent/confident language
7Detailed knowledge; acknowledges structure/language; sustained, well-supported response; accurate/appropriate language
6Clear knowledge; some elements of ideas; some structure/language awareness; supported by evidence; mostly accurate language
5Adequate knowledge; some reference to ideas; understanding of topic with evidence; adequate communication
4Basic knowledge; limited reference to ideas; some topic understanding; adequate expression
3Familiarity with text; limited topic awareness; basic expression
1–2Limited familiarity; very limited topic awareness; unclear language
0No knowledge of text / no engagement with topic / minimal language control
Section B
Creating a Text
Create an original text responding to a Framework of Ideas title and at least one piece of stimulus material. Any prose form is acceptable. Marked out of 10.
0 words
Click to upload a photo of handwritten work — Claude will transcribe it
Assessment Criteria:
  • Use of relevant ideas from a Framework of Ideas, the title, and at least one piece of stimulus material
  • Creation of a cohesive text with clear purpose(s) and appropriate voice
  • Use of suitable text structure(s) and language features
  • Fluent expression and appropriate vocabulary
Expected Qualities:
9–10Insightful Framework ideas; cohesive text with explicit purpose and voice; sophisticated language/structure control; rich vocabulary
8Astute exploration of Framework ideas; coherent text; confident language control; thoughtful vocabulary
7Detailed connection to Framework ideas; coordinated text; sound control; clear vocabulary
6Clear connection; connected text with clear purpose and voice; effective vocabulary
5Adequate connection; organised text; adequate control
4Basic connection; some purpose/voice awareness; basic control
3Limited connection; limited purpose or voice awareness; limited language/structure
1–2Little/no connection; little awareness of purpose; unclear language
0No engagement with the task
Section C
Analysing Argument
Analyse how written and spoken language and visuals are used to present arguments and persuade the audience. This is an analytical response, not a persuasive essay. Marked out of 10.
0 words

Assessment Criteria:
  • Understanding of contention, argument(s), and point of view
  • Analysis of the ways written/spoken language and visuals are used to present arguments and persuade the intended audience
  • Use of evidence from the text to support the analysis
  • Fluent expression and appropriate vocabulary / Standard Australian English
Expected Qualities:
9–10Perceptive understanding of contention/argument; sophisticated insight into HOW language/visuals persuade; sophisticated/precise language
8Thoughtful understanding; sound insight into how language/visuals build argument; confident language
7Detailed understanding; insight into persuasive techniques; fluent expression
6Clear understanding; some awareness of persuasive language/visuals; competent expression
5Adequate understanding; basic awareness of techniques; adequate language
4Basic understanding; describes rather than analyses; basic language
3Limited knowledge of argument or point of view; limited technique knowledge
1–2Little understanding; minimal task knowledge; unclear language
0No understanding of task requirements
