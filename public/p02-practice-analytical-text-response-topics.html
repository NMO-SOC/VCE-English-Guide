Part 02 · Sunset BoulevardChapter 1 of 11

Practice Analytical Text Response Topics

1. ‘Maybe that’s why it’s good, it’s true” Sunset Boulevard shows that the truth, however ordinary or painful, is what matters. Do you agree? 2. In Billy Wilder’s Sunset Boulevard things are never what they seem. Discuss. 3. Wilder’s Sunset Boulevard opposes exploitation in all its forms. Discuss. 4. Sunset Boulevard explores different kinds of madness. Discuss. 5. ‘There’s nothing tragic about being 50, not unless you try to be 25”. Sunset Boulevard is about the need to face reality. Do you agree? 6. In Sunset Boulevard fantasy prevents the truth from being confronted. To what extent do you agree? 7. ‘Sagittarius! I like Sagittarians - you can trust them” Sunset Boulevard warns against trusting others. Discuss. 8. ‘I felt trapped like the cigarette in the contraption on her finger”. Sunset Boulevard is concerned with entrapment. Do you agree? 9. Sunset Boulevard explores the intermingling of public and private realms. Discuss. 10. Billy Wilder’s Sunset Boulevard explores the intersection of the written and the real. Discuss. 11. Sunset Boulevard touches on the true horrors of Hollywood. Do you agree? 12. The structure of Sunset Boulevard is crucial to its message. Discuss. 13. Sunset Boulevard is not just a sad tale of deception, greed, and jealousy. Discuss. 14. ‘I am big, it’s the pictures that got small’ Sunset Boulevard shows that mistaken beliefs are responsible for suffering. Do you agree? 15. Sunset Boulevard argues that we have little control over our lives. To what extent do you agree? 16. ‘But before you hear it all distorted and blown out of proportion, before those Hollywood columnists get their hands on it, maybe you’d like to hear the facts, the whole truth”. In Sunset Boulevard truth is the real victim. Discuss. 17. Sunset Boulevard reveals Hollywood as a world of predation and exploitation. Do you agree? 18. Sunset Boulevard explores the consequences of people being in the wrong place or time. Discuss. 19. Billy Wilder’s Sunset Boulevard is a contest between selfishness and self-sacrifice. Discuss. 20. Norma and Joe are very similar people. To what extend Do you agree? 21. How does Wilder create suspense for the audience in Sunset Boulevard? 22. “I remember waving to you. I was dancing on a table.” Sunset Boulevard shows the lengths people will go to in order to keep the past alive. Discuss. 23. “Look sweetie, be practical.” Joe’s pursuit of fame is a symptom of the selfish society he inhabits. To what extent do you agree? 24. How does Wilder’s choice of a noir thriller position viewers to feel uncomfortable in Sunset Boulevard? 25. “That’s the trouble with you readers, you know all the plots.” ‘In the world of Sunset Boulevard, it is impossible to experience anything true.’ Do you agree? 26. ‘In Sunset Boulevard, the magic of the movies carries also the potential for disenchantment.’ Discuss. 27. ‘Joe’s death is the result of his failure to see the tragic nature of his relationship with Norma.’ Do you agree? 28. ‘Sunset Boulevard represents a condemnation of Hollywood’s idea of itself as the dream factory.’ To what extent do you agree? 29. ‘Sunset Boulevard exposes the less than benevolent treatment of creative talent in the pursuit of profit.’ Discuss. 30. Wilder’s film is a scathing critique of the Hollywood film industry. To what extent do you agree? 31. How does Wilder use Franz Waxman’s score to intensify the events of the film? 32. Joe Gillis was complicit in all the events of Sunset Boulevard and deserves no sympathy. Discuss. 33. Norma Desmond is a tragic victim of a cruel industry. Discuss. 34. How does Wilder’s use of a voiceover contribute to the menacing nature of the film? 35. “I am big. It’s the pictures that got small.” Sunset Boulevard is an examination of the dangers of pride. To what extent do you agree? 36. Norma Desmond’s delusions are fuelled and supported by men hoping to profit from her fame and fortune. Discuss. 37. In Wilder’s Sunset Boulevard, every character is selfish and opportunistic. To what extent do you agree? 38. How does Wilder capture the power of delusion in his film Sunset Boulevard? 39. “DeMille didn’t have the heart to tell you. None of us had the heart.” How does deception contribute to the tragic events of Sunset Boulevard?

Topics from Previous Exams (VCAA and others)

1. ‘In Sunset Boulevard it is the capacity for self-delusion, rather than fame and wealth, that is shown to be destructive.’ Discuss. 2. ‘All the characters in Sunset Boulevard allow themselves to be entrapped.’ To what extent do you agree? 3. Sunset Boulevard shows the corrupt and corrupting side of Hollywood.’ Do you agree? 4. ‘Sunset Boulevard is as funny and absurd as it is tragic.’ Discuss. 5. ‘We can pity the characters in Sunset Boulevard, but we cannot like or admire them.’ Do you agree? 6. ‘Joe and other characters do see a real if faded glamour in Norma Desmond.’ Discuss. 7. ‘Sunset Boulevard highlights the importance of adapting to new circumstances.’ Discuss. 8. “Great stars have great pride.” To what extent does Norma cause her own downfall? 9. ‘Sunset Boulevard exposes Hollywood as a cruel and exploitative environment.’ Discuss. 10. “I made her a star and I cannot let her be destroyed.”‘It is Max who bears the most responsibility for Norma’s descent into madness.’ Do you agree? 11. ‘In this film, the rife presence of discontent underscores the predatory and vicious nature of Hollywood.’ Discuss. 12. "All right, Mr. DeMille, I’m ready for my close-up.”‘By the end of Sunset Boulevard, the audience is left feeling nothing but sympathy for Norma.’ To what extent do you agree? 13. How is death narratively and symbolically integral to Sunset Boulevard? 14. ‘In this film, the distinctions between fantasy and reality are blurred.’ Discuss. 15. ‘Max’s actions are to blame for Norma’s dangerous detachment from reality.’ Do you agree? 16. ‘Deception affects the exterior and interior lives of the characters.’ Discuss. 17. ‘Norma’s obsession with her own celebrity status is detrimental to herself and others.’ Discuss. 18. ‘Relationships in Sunset Boulevard are not completely devoid of love.’ To what extent do you agree?
