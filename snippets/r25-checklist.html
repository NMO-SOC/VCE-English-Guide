Priority Checklist for 2026

The habits below fall straight out of the 2025 report — each one addresses something assessors explicitly rewarded or penalised.

Before the exam

  • Build a topic-interpretation routine: identify the conceptual focus, the command term, the verb, and at least two implications before planning.
  • Rehearse unfamiliar-verb strategy — and pack a dictionary.
  • Practise writing to a fixed title in Section B without bending a pre-prepared piece to fit.
  • For Section C practice, always start from the background information and write down audience, purpose and author positioning before analysing.

In the exam

  • Shade every box: text and topic (Section A); Framework and stimulus (Section B) — and write the Section B title first.
  • Section A: open and close each paragraph with sentences that link your ideas to the topic; use the conclusion to resolve the question.
  • Section B: keep the title in view as you write; make every structural and word choice serve your purpose and reader.
  • Section C: select the few elements most central to the persuasion and explain their interplay — argument, language and visuals together.
  • Everywhere: sophisticated language only where it communicates a sophisticated idea.
