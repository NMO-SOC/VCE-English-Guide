Part 01

How to use this site

Everything from the 2026 exam booklet, organised so you can find what you need in seconds. Here’s the lay of the land.

Search anything

The search box in the sidebar covers every section of the guide — type a character, theme, scene or skill (e.g. Norma, symbols, rebuttal) and jump straight there.

Browse by part

The numbered sidebar mirrors the booklet’s 12 parts. Each page has an “On this page” box at the top so you can skip to any subsection.

Practice exams built in

Past papers and practice exams are viewable right on the page — or open them in a new tab / download them for timed practice on paper.

Exemplars index

The home page lists all 15 high-marking sample responses in one place, each linking straight to the essay.

Where to go for each exam section

Section ASunset Boulevard or Rainbow’s End — context, quote banks, scene analyses, symbols and sample essays — then how to build the essay.
Section BCreating Texts — audience, purpose, the Personal Journeys framework and practice stimuli.
Section CAnalysing Argument — essay structure, written and visual language analysis, practice articles and sample essays.

Suggested routes

Weeks out from the exam

Work through your text’s quote banks and scene analyses, then study the exemplar essays against the marking criteria. Build a study plan with Effectively Studying for Exams.

Practising under conditions

Sit a paper from Practice Exams, then self-mark with the assessment criteria and check the 2024 assessor feedback for common pitfalls.

Final days

Run the Exam Checklists, revisit The Exam for format and timing strategy, and skim your quote banks one last time.
