General Exam Advice

The 2025 examination produced near-identical averages across all three sections — 5.4, 5.4 and 5.5 out of 10 — with very few students at either extreme. The middle is crowded; the marks that separate a 5 from an 8 come from the specific skills assessors described, not from writing more.

SectionAverage /10What it was
A5.4Analytical response to a text
B5.4Creating a text (Frameworks of Ideas)
C5.5Analysis of argument and language

How assessors marked

  • Holistic, criteria-based, benchmarked. Every section was assessed against the published criteria and expected qualities (EQs), using benchmark scripts to set standards. The three EQs are interconnected: the quality of your ideas (EQ1) sets the level of challenge for structuring and substantiating them (EQ2), and language (EQ3) is judged by how well it communicates those ideas.
  • Everything is applied in relation to the topic. Sound ideas, apt quotations or fluent expression that do not serve the set task cannot score highly. This principle recurs through every section of the report.
  • First-draft conditions were respected. Assessors explicitly acknowledged that exam writing is timed first-draft work; fluency was not equated with perfection.

Administrative habits that mattered

  • Shade the box for your chosen text and topic in Section A, and for your Framework and stimulus in Section B — and write the Section B title at the start of your response.
  • Choose the topic that best lets you demonstrate your knowledge and skills, not the one you predicted.
  • Bring a dictionary. Assessors specifically noted students with no strategy for unfamiliar words in topics — some simply substituted "and" for a term they didn't understand, which derailed their essays.
